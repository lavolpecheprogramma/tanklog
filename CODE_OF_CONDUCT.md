# Code of Conduct

TankLog is an open-source project. We want participation to be a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in language and actions.
- Being open to feedback and different viewpoints.
- Focusing on what’s best for the community and project.
- Showing empathy towards other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others’ private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is representing the project or its community in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the maintainer using the contact information available on the project’s GitHub page/profile.

If you are comfortable doing so, you may also open an issue describing the situation (please avoid sharing sensitive personal information in public).

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.
